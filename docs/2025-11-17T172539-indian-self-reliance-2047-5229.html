Self-reliance is not merely about being independent but encompasses the ability to sustain oneself in terms of economy, innovation, and culture. It means creating an ecosystem that fosters local industries, embraces sustainable practices, and empowers individuals. In the context of India, self-reliance translates to being resilient against global shocks and ensuring economic stability for future generations.

Key Initiatives for Self Reliance

Make in India

The 'Make in India' initiative launched in 2014, encourages companies to manufacture their products in India. It has provided a significant boost to the manufacturing sector and has led to job creation, making the country a hub for various industries from electronics to textiles.

Digital India

With the Digital India initiative, the country is embracing technological advancements to improve governance and empower citizens. This initiative not only focuses on providing digital infrastructure but also on increasing digital literacy, which ultimately enhances the potential of a self-reliant economy.

Atmanirbhar Bharat

In 2020, the 'Atmanirbhar Bharat' or Self-reliant India campaign was launched as a response to the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic. This initiative is focused on increasing domestic production, mitigating import reliance, and encouraging local consumption. By investing in local industries, India aims to create a robust economy that can withstand global uncertainties.

Impacts of Self Reliance on Citizens

This movement towards self-reliance encourages individuals to become more conscious of their consumption patterns and to prioritize locally produced goods.
